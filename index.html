Singapore Bunker Market

The Client: Independent Asian refiner wanted to launch bunker trading in 2013 which would require securing long - term storage leases from independent terminal, hiring staff, deploying barges and building up clients to world’s largest marine fuels market.

Work: Delivered comprehensive assessment of capital required, staffing, risks and returns for Client and multiple scenarios for market participation including non - entry. Major identified concerns included

Outcome: After reviewing findings from JTD, the Client decided not to enter the Singapore bunker market in 2013. JTD’s services to the Client ended.

Epilouge: In late 2014, the Singapore marine fuels market suffered a major blow with the insolvency of the world’s 7th largest bunker supplier, OW Bunker. Client dodged a bullet

Action: Delivered comprehensive analysis and explanation of relevant industry price assessment and valuation models at more than 30% under Client’s capped fee.

Epilouge: Report received a favourable response from the Client and court testimony from JTD was not required

Risk Management

The Client: Small, independent Asian refinery recently spun off by large integrated energy firm needed to setup risk management programme, including staff training, risk monitoring and hedging.

Background:the Client had no experience with hedging and derivatives as their former owner had performed these functions. The Client needed an intensive, customized 2 full day course to explain the basics of risk management and develop recommended strategies for hedging their refinery margins. Under Non-Disclosure Agreements, the Client provided JTD with proprietary documents on the purchase and supply agreements and refinery operations

Action: Within 2 weeks from the initial contact from the Client, JTD delivered complete 2—day training package for their entire staff from the Chief Executive Officer to operations assistance on the various risk management strategies the Client might consider. In response to Client’s request on longer dated strategy to hedge refinery margin risk from early 2017 and beyond, JTD recommended that Client aggressively hedge markets in anticipation of market structure shifting into steeper backwardation. This strategy proved to be sound

Epilouge: Client was pleased with course and eventually recruited experienced risk manager.

Launch of Oil Trading Operation

Work:Since 2013 JTD has developed and delivered over forty (40) days of training coursework including exercises, simulations, and interactive team-based negotiations meeting Client’s requirement.

Epilouge: In 2016, the Client spun off the Energy Price Risk Management as a standalone professional two-day course which has been presented successfully 3 times per year to the present day. The Commodity course for Skills Future credit was successfully launched in 2018 and continues to the present day. Many former students have found employment or received promotions in the energy industry and maintain post-course contact with JTD.
